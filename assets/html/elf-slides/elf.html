Conceptual illustration of ELF (Fig. 2)

Orange points: data in continuous embedding space. Purple lines: denoising trajectories from Gaussian noise to clean embeddings. Discretization is applied only at t=1, via a shared-weight network.

Trajectories preserve y-rank across panels — flow lines do not cross. Animated purple dots trace the flow continuously.

3D view of the flow (animated)

The 2D figure overloads the X-axis with both embedding X and time. In reality the structure is a 3D tube: each slice is a 2D embedding plane (y, z), and the horizontal direction across slices is the time axis t.

Panels are tilted (oblique) to suggest 2D slices viewed at an angle; the only horizontal motion is along t.

Continuous diffusion in action (real ELF samples)

Three unconditional samples, decoded at each of 101 intermediate sampling steps (from t=0 noise to t=1 tokens, every 1% of the schedule). Text fades from light noise to dark final as t grows.

Decoder applied at every intermediate step here, only for visualization; during real sampling decoding happens once at t=1.

An example: how does ELF generate a sentence?

training 1/4 Tokens → continuous embeddings

Each discrete token \( s_i \) is encoded into a continuous embedding \( \mathbf{x}_i \in \mathbb{R}^d \) by a (frozen) T5 encoder. The encoder is used only at training time.

training 2/4 Corrupt clean x to noisy zt

Sample t ∈ [0,1] from a logit-normal schedule and Gaussian noise ε. Linear interpolation (rectified flow): \( \mathbf{z}_t \,=\, t\,\mathbf{x} \,+\, (1-t)\,\boldsymbol{\epsilon} \) Target velocity: \( \mathbf{v} = \mathbf{x} - \boldsymbol{\epsilon} \).

training 3/4 ELF predicts the clean x̂  (MSE loss)

Network outputs x-prediction \( \hat{\mathbf{x}}_\theta = \mathrm{net}_\theta(\mathbf{z}_t, t) \). Loss reparameterizes to \( \mathcal{L}_{\text{MSE}} \,=\, \mathbb{E}\,\frac{1}{(1-t)^2}\,\| \hat{\mathbf{x}}_\theta - \mathbf{x} \|^2 \) x-prediction is what makes the next step's weight sharing possible.

training 4/4 Decode branch at t=1  (CE loss)

Same shared-weight network, mode = decode. A per-token corruption keeps the input non-trivial; logits = W·x̂; supervised by \( \mathcal{L}_{\text{CE}} \,=\, \mathbb{E}\big[\,\mathrm{CE}(W\hat{\mathbf{x}}_\theta(\tilde{\mathbf{z}}),\,\mathbf{s})\,\big] \)

sampling 1/2 Iteratively denoise zt → zt+1

At each step: x-prediction \( \hat{\mathbf{x}}_\theta \) → velocity \( \hat{\mathbf{v}} = (\hat{\mathbf{x}}_\theta - \mathbf{z}_t)/(1-t) \) → Euler step \( \mathbf{z}_{t+\Delta t} \,=\, \mathbf{z}_t \,+\, \Delta t \cdot \hat{\mathbf{v}} \) The same ELF network is called repeatedly; cells get cleaner as t approaches 1.

sampling 2/2 Final step t=1: decode to tokens

Switch the same network to decode mode at t=1, apply the unembedding matrix W, then take argmax per token. No separate decoder.
